During my doctoral and postdoctoral training, I worked at several research institutes in Rosario, Santa Fe, Argentina, including:

  • Cancer Research Center of Rosario (CIC-CIPReB)
  • Institute of Clinical and Experimental Immunology of Rosario (IDICER-CONICET)
  • Rosario Max Planck Laboratory (MPLbioR)

My research primarily focused on large-scale transcriptomic data analysis, leveraging advanced programming tools in R and specialized packages from Bioconductor.
Key aspects of my work included:

  • Data processing: Handling raw data in various formats (.CEL, .fastq) from public repositories (e.g., GEO, TCGA) and private projects.
  • Statistical analysis: Applying parametric/non-parametric methods (linear models, hypothesis testing, classification algorithms) to identify biological patterns. Performed differential expression analysis using tools like DESeq2, edgeR, and limma-voom for RNA-seq and microarray data. Conducted Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA) and Overrepresentation Analysis (ORA) to identify significantly enriched pathways and biological functions.
  • Network analysis & visualization: Using tools like igraph, ggplot2, and Cytoscape to derive insights and communicate findings.
