I have experience applying Machine Learning techniques to the analysis of transcriptomic data, enabling the study of gene activity at a large scale in biological systems. My work in this area has covered different stages:

Preprocessing and Dimensionality Reduction
I have experience in data cleaning and normalization to ensure quality and comparability. I have applied dimensionality reduction methods such as Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE) to explore patterns and structures in high-dimensional datasets. For these tasks, I have used tools like scikit-learn (Python), Seurat, and FactoMineR (R).

Feature Selection
I have experience optimizing predictive models by applying feature selection techniques to identify relevant variables in noisy datasets. For this stage, I have worked with libraries such as scikit-learn and caret.

Predictive Modeling with Supervised Learning
I have built classification and prediction models using algorithms such as Lasso regression, Random Forest, and Support Vector Machines (SVM). I am proficient with libraries like glmnet, randomForest, and e1071 in R.

Model Validation and Evaluation
To ensure the robustness and generalization of models, I have applied cross-validation techniques (k-fold cross-validation) and evaluated model performance using ROC curves and metrics such as the Area Under the Curve (AUC). For this purpose, I have worked with libraries like pROC and ROCR.

My experience in this field has focused on combining Machine Learning principles with a strong understanding of molecular biology, allowing me to extract meaningful insights from transcriptomic data and contribute to research focused on biomarker discovery, diagnostics, and personalized medicine.
