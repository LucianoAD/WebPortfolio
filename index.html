About Me

My initial training was focused on the field of biology and health, where I began my career working in cancer genetics. During this time, I immersed myself in manipulating and analyzing extensive data tables, challenging the limitations of conventional tools. This challenge led me to explore the world of programming, learning languages such as R and Python, as well as relational database management and artificial intelligence techniques.

I have collaborated on data analysis for various scientific research projects, significantly contributing to the identification of key genes in disease treatment. I am currently also a professor in a program focused on data analysis with artificial intelligence.

Throughout these experiences, I have come to understand that data analysis skills are cross-cutting across any industry. I have developed a deep interest in expanding my horizons to new topics and challenges.
